# The Moribund Institute's Code of Conduct for Coding Projects

## Our Mission

Our primary goal is the rapid acceleration and execution of ideas. We value working code, clean architecture, and technical excellence over social pleasantries.

## The Rules of Engagement

**Code Wins Arguments:** We care about the quality of the pull request, not the pedigree or the ego of the coder. Technical merits decide what gets merged (admittedly, this is decided arbitrarily by humans, but at least it's grounded in real empirical tests). This is coding, after all, not the social sciences with synthetic models of what-if scenarios.

**Directness Is Expected:** Blunt, critical technical feedback is a feature, not a bug. Do not confuse a harsh code review with a personal attack. We expect contributors to have thick skins regarding their code.

**Genius Must Be Maintainable:** A magical solution that nobody else can read or maintain is a liability, not an asset. If you write brilliant code, you are expected to document it. We like the Feynman technique here: if you can't explain your code simply, you probably don't understand it, or you had AI write too much of it for you & didn't investigate what it wrote.

**No Time-Wasting Drama:** We tolerate eccentricities and blunt communication, but we do not tolerate active harassment, doxxing, or deliberate attempts to sabotage the community. Drama is friction. Friction slows down development.

## Enforcement

The maintainer's time is the most valuable resource in this project. If you create social friction that outweighs the technical value of your code, your contributions will be rejected and you will be removed from the repository.
